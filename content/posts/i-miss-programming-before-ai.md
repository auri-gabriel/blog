## Something Changed

With the rapid progress of LLMs, developers are writing less code manually each day. This might be good if you are in the software engineering industry mainly for the money, but it can feel disappointing if you genuinely enjoy writing code the old-fashioned way.

The lightbulb moments just don't happen as often anymore.

## When I Fell in Love With Programming

I started my Software Engineering degree in 2019. As of today, I still haven't graduated, but during the first four and a half years of my studies, when I was mostly focused on coursework, I fell in love with computers and their languages.

There was nothing more exciting than the moment when a solution suddenly appeared in my mind after struggling with a problem.

However, in the last two years, since I started working professionally, those moments have become rarer.

## The Fun Part of Programming

Many people talk about the atrophy of knowledge when relying on LLMs. While that might be true, for me the main issue is different.

Accepting a solution generated by an LLM simply isn’t as exciting as building the solution myself.

The process of thinking, experimenting, and finally discovering the answer was a big part of what made programming enjoyable.

## The Way Things Change

This post is really about how things change.

I still love solving problems, and that is something I continue to do even with the help of AI tools. But I will always miss the feeling of coding and discovering solutions the way I used to in the past.
